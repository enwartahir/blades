# Teyvat Blades — Website Data & Story Bible

---

## The World

Long before the Archons rose to power. Long before Mondstadt had walls or Liyue had a harbor. Long before any traveler ever crossed between worlds — there were the blades.

Forged in the age of fire and myth, tempered by the tears of dying gods, sharpened on the bones of ancient beasts that no living creature has ever seen. Each sword is not merely a weapon. It is a relic. A memory. A fragment of a world that no longer exists in the form it once took.

They have passed through a thousand hands. They have split mountains, parted oceans, ended dynasties. They have been lost, buried, stolen, reforged, shattered, and reborn. And now, somehow, impossibly — they are here.

**This is their story.**

## The Ten Swords — Full Stories

---

### SWORD 01 — BAKUFU SHADES
**Element:** Electro (Purple)
**Rarity:** ★★★★ (4 Star)
**Origin:** Inazuma — The Shogunate Era
**ATK:** 41 | **Substat:** Elemental Mastery +12

#### The Story

The Raiden Shogun sealed the borders of Inazuma for five hundred years. In that time, the island nation turned inward, and its craftsmen — denied access to the outside world — became the finest weapon-makers in all of Teyvat. They had nothing to do but perfect their art.

Bakufu Shades was made by a swordsmith named Taro Sado who never left his forge for eleven years. He believed that the sword was already there, hiding inside the steel — that his job was not to create but to reveal. He spoke to the metal as he worked. He asked it what it wanted to be.

What it wanted to be was a conductor.

The blade carries a permanent charge — not from Vision or enchantment, but from the steel itself, which was quenched not in water but in the storm runoff collected from the highest peak of Yashiori Island during the three-hour window when lightning strikes continuously. The resulting blade hums at a frequency that sets teeth on edge and makes dogs bark three streets away.

It was carried by twelve Shogunate officers over the centuries. Seven of them were struck by lightning during their service. None of them died — the blade grounded the strike, channeling it harmlessly through the wielder and into the earth. The remaining five retired and reported the same dream: standing at the center of a storm, perfectly calm, perfectly alive.

Taro Sado died at his forge at the age of ninety-one, still working. They found him slumped over a new blade, hands still wrapped around the handle. His apprentices swore the unfinished sword was still warm.

**Tagline:** *"The storm does not choose who it strikes. The blade does."*

### SWORD 02 — BOREAS
**Element:** Anemo (Teal/Green)
**Rarity:** ★★★★ (4 Star)
**Origin:** Mondstadt — The Age of Storms
**ATK:** 44 | **Substat:** ATK +6%

#### The Story

Before Mondstadt was a city of freedom and wine, it was a city of wind and war. The storms that swept off the sea were not gentle breezes — they were walls of fury that leveled settlements and drove sailors mad. The ancient Mondstadtians did not celebrate the wind. They survived it.

Boreas was named not after a god but after the season — the deep winter gales that came from the north and lasted three months without pause. A warrior named Hauk the Unbroken commissioned the blade from a traveling smith who claimed he could capture wind inside metal. Most people thought this was a fool's errand. Hauk paid him anyway.

The smith disappeared into the mountains for forty days. He returned with Boreas, and he was half the weight he had been when he left. He never explained what the process required. He never made another weapon.

The blade is unsettling in a way that takes time to notice. It is always slightly cold to the touch, regardless of temperature. When held at arm's length in still air, a faint current moves around it — not quite a breeze, something subtler, as if the air itself is paying attention. When swung, it makes a sound that is difficult to describe: not a whistle, not a hum, but something that feels like a word spoken just below the threshold of hearing.

The Boreas Wolf — the great wind spirit that Venti once contended with — is said to have recognized the blade when it was raised against it. The wolf stopped, tilted its enormous head, and let out a sound that witnesses described as something between a growl and a laugh. Then it attacked anyway.

**Tagline:** *"The north wind does not ask permission. Neither does the blade that carries its name."*

### SWORD 03 — COOL STEEL
**Element:** Hydro (Blue)
**Rarity:** ★★★ (3 Star)
**Origin:** Liyue — The Harbor Era
**ATK:** 39 | **Substat:** ATK +7.7%

#### The Story

Not every legend begins in fire and drama. Some begin in a quiet workshop in the lower districts of Liyue Harbor, where a woman named Wei Mingzhu made knives for forty years without ever making a single weapon she considered worth naming.

Cool Steel was made on a Tuesday. Wei Mingzhu didn't feel inspired that morning. She didn't have a vision. She simply sat down at her bench with good steel she'd been saving and did what she always did: she worked carefully, she worked patiently, and she refused to cut corners on any step, no matter how small.

The result was a sword of such unremarkable perfection that it confused people who held it. There was no magic in it. There were no runes, no enchantments, no unusual metals. It was simply flawless — balanced to a tolerance that instrument makers measured three times because they didn't believe the first reading. The edge could part a single hair lengthwise. The spine would not flex under four hundred kilograms of lateral pressure.

Wei Mingzhu sold it to a merchant for a fair price. The merchant sold it to a soldier. The soldier carried it through eight campaigns and thirty-seven individual engagements and never once had to sharpen it. He gave it to his daughter. She gave it to her student. The chain of custody runs unbroken for two hundred years.

Every person who has carried Cool Steel has said a version of the same thing: it makes you better. Not through any mystical influence — it simply refuses to fail you. And when your weapon refuses to fail you, you stop fearing failure. And when you stop fearing failure, you become something else entirely.

Wei Mingzhu never knew any of this. She died thinking she had made nothing remarkable.

**Tagline:** *"Reliability is its own kind of magic. Some blades don't need to be famous. They just need to work."*

### SWORD 04 — DULL BLADE
**Element:** None
**Rarity:** ★ (1 Star)
**Origin:** Unknown — Everywhere
**ATK:** 23 | **Substat:** —

#### The Story

There is no story here. Not yet.

This is the blade that has no history because it has not yet made one. It has been in a hundred pawnshops, a hundred training yards, a hundred the-night-before moments when someone picked it up because it was all they had.

It is dented. One side of the guard is slightly bent. The grip wrap has been replaced so many times that the original material is a mystery. There is a hairline crack near the base of the blade that by all rights should have caused it to snap years ago, and somehow hasn't.

It is, on every quantifiable metric, a bad sword.

And yet.

There is something in the weight of a weapon that has been held by desperate hands. Something in a blade that has seen a hundred beginnings. The Dull Blade does not remember the warriors who failed with it — history doesn't record them. But it was also in the hand of someone, somewhere, who picked it up with nothing but stubbornness and came out the other side of something impossible.

You don't know which story you're in until the end.

Every master was once a beginner. Every legendary blade was once an unproven one. The Dull Blade is not a weapon. It is a question.

*What are you willing to do with what you have?*

**Tagline:** *"It is not the blade that is dull. It is the story — and stories, unlike steel, can be sharpened."*

### SWORD 05 — FREEDOM-SWORN
**Element:** Anemo (Teal/Gold)
**Rarity:** ★★★★★ (5 Star)
**Origin:** Mondstadt — The Rebellion Era
**ATK:** 46 | **Substat:** Elemental Mastery +43

#### The Story

The Aristocracy ruled Mondstadt for four hundred years. They did not rule well.

The freedom that Mondstadt now celebrates — the festivals, the wine, the wind that moves through open streets without obstruction — was purchased at a price that the history books understate. There was blood. There were executions in the town square. There were years when the word "freedom" could get you killed for speaking it above a whisper.

Freedom-Sworn was made in secret, in a cellar, by a coalition of rebel smiths who had lost family members to the Aristocracy. They pooled their finest materials and their combined knowledge, and they worked for three years without stopping. Every person who contributed to the blade swore the same oath over it — an oath not of vengeance, not of hatred, but of purpose:

*"We do not fight for ourselves. We fight for the world we want to exist after we are gone."*

The blade absorbs that oath. This is not a metaphor. Something in the forging process — the collective intention, the shared grief, the years of slow careful work in the dark — changed the metal at a molecular level that Sumeru scholars still cannot fully explain. The blade resonates with elemental energy in a way that amplifies the wielder's power through the force of their conviction rather than through their strength.

The weaker your anger, the stronger the blade makes you. The purer your purpose, the more devastating each strike. A warrior of petty motivations will find it an ordinary sword. A warrior who truly believes in something larger than themselves will hold something that terrifies gods.

Venti knows this sword. He was there when it was made. He has never spoken publicly about what happened in that cellar during those three years, and when pressed, he changes the subject with uncharacteristic speed.

**Tagline:** *"It does not swing harder for those who fight in anger. It swings hardest for those who fight so that others will never have to."*

### SWORD 06 — KATANA
**Element:** Pyro (Orange/Red)
**Rarity:** ★★★★ (4 Star)
**Origin:** Inazuma — The Dueling Tradition
**ATK:** 41 | **Substat:** ATK +12%

#### The Story

In Inazuma, the katana is not a weapon. It is a philosophy made physical.

The smith who forged this particular blade was a woman named Izumi who trained for twenty years before she ever touched metal. She studied the way water falls. She studied the way fire moves. She sat for six months watching the forge fires of her master without being allowed to approach them, learning to read heat the way a musician learns to read silence.

When she finally made her first sword, it took her forty-seven attempts over two years to produce one she considered acceptable. That sword was given away to a traveler who passed through and needed a weapon. She never saw it again. She felt this was correct.

This katana — her forty-seventh attempt, the first one she didn't immediately melt down — took eleven days to complete. She worked without sleeping, subsisting on water and cold rice, and she spoke to no one. The forge burned hotter than it had any right to burn. Neighbors reported seeing light through the cracks in the building walls that seemed to pulse with a rhythm like breathing.

The finished blade holds heat differently than other steel. Not enough to burn — you can grip it bare-handed — but enough that on cold mornings, you can feel the warmth of it through the sheath. In darkness, there is a faint orange luminescence at the core of the blade, barely visible, like an ember that has never gone out.

Izumi carried it herself for the remainder of her life. She never entered a formal duel. She never killed anyone. She used it to cut vegetables in her kitchen until she died at sixty-eight, peacefully, in the same house where she built the forge.

Her students searched the house afterward and found three things: the katana, her notes on the philosophy of the forge, and a small drawing of a flame she had made the day before she died. The flame in the drawing is smiling. This detail disturbs swordsmiths more than they are comfortable admitting.

**Tagline:** *"The blade is not the weapon. The discipline that made the blade — that is the weapon."*

### SWORD 07 — LION'S ROAR
**Element:** Pyro (Orange/Gold)
**Rarity:** ★★★★ (4 Star)
**Origin:** Liyue — The Adepti Wars
**ATK:** 42 | **Substat:** ATK +9%

#### The Story

The great lion guardian of Liyue has many names in different eras of the harbor's history. In the oldest texts — the ones written in the script that even most Liyue scholars can't fully read — it appears as a judge. A force that evaluated the worth of those who asked for its protection and found most of them wanting.

Lion's Roar was not commissioned by a hero. It was commissioned by the lion itself, speaking through an Adeptus intermediary, because the lion had grown tired of humans bringing inadequate weapons to battles that required adequate ones. The Adeptus was uncomfortable with this assignment. The lion was not sympathetic.

The blade was forged to a specification the lion communicated in a dream — specific proportions, specific temperatures, a specific sequence of hammer strikes that must be performed in sets of eight with a pause of exactly four seconds between each set. The Adeptus who forged it spent six months attempting to understand why these specifications mattered before giving up on understanding and simply following them.

The result is a sword that has unusual effects on demon-class entities. Not enhanced damage, exactly — the effect is more psychological. Demons that encounter the blade hesitate. The strongest ones pause before attacking. The weakest ones run. Scholars theorize it produces a sub-audible frequency that resonates with demonic consciousness as something fundamentally threatening, the way certain smells trigger primal fear responses.

The lion, when asked about this, gave an answer that was translated as: "I told it to sound like me. It listened." 

**Tagline:** *"A demon that hesitates is a demon that loses. This blade makes them hesitate."*

### SWORD 08 — MISTSPLITTER REFORGED
**Element:** Cryo (Ice Blue/Silver)
**Rarity:** ★★★★★ (5 Star)
**Origin:** Inazuma — Pre-Archon Era
**ATK:** 48 | **Substat:** CRIT DMG +9.6%

#### The Story

There was an original Mistsplitter. It no longer exists in any form that can be touched or seen.

The original was forged in an age before the Archons, when the boundaries between the physical world and the realm of the dead were thinner — not metaphorically thin, but physically thin, places where the barrier could be felt as a texture if you reached in the right direction. The original Mistsplitter was used to cut through such a place. Used to create a passage.

What came through that passage is not recorded. What is recorded is that the sword shattered in the process, and that the pieces fell on both sides of the boundary simultaneously. For three hundred years, twelve fragments existed in the physical world, scattered across Inazuma's islands. The other twelve were somewhere else.

A Shogunate general named Koharu Sato spent thirty years collecting the twelve physical fragments. She found the last one at the bottom of the ocean, in a place so cold and dark that the diving specialist she hired came back up with frostbitten fingers and a permanent terror of deep water. Koharu herself went down alone the next day and retrieved it without incident. When asked how, she said only: "I was not afraid of what was down there. I was afraid of leaving it unfinished."

A master smith reforged the twelve pieces into one. The blade is not quite the original — it cannot be, because the other twelve pieces are still somewhere else, and a thing that is only half-present is not quite the same as a whole thing. But it is not diminished by this. It is changed.

The reforged blade cuts through more than physical matter. Psychic barriers, emotional walls, the sensation of being trapped — in proximity to Mistsplitter Reforged, these things become less solid. Warriors who carry it report vivid dreams of vast cold spaces, blue light, the feeling of being at the edge of something infinite. Most find this comforting. Some find it terrifying. The difference seems to lie in whether you fear what is infinite or whether you are curious about it.

Koharu carried the reforged blade until she died. She died alone, in a field, at the age of eighty-four, facing north. No one knows why north.

**Tagline:** *"Half of it exists here. Half of it exists somewhere else. All of it cuts."*

### SWORD 09 — NARUKAMI
**Element:** Electro (Deep Purple/Gold)
**Rarity:** ★★★★★ (5 Star)
**Origin:** Inazuma — The Archon Era
**ATK:** 46 | **Substat:** CRIT Rate +7.2%

#### The Story

The Electro Archon does not give gifts. She enforces. She judges. She endures.

But once — only once, in five hundred years of ruling from the Tenshukaku — she descended to the forge district of Inazuma city in the form of an ordinary woman with no entourage and no announcement, and she asked a swordsmith for two hours of his time.

The swordsmith, a man named Kajiro, recognized her immediately. He said nothing about this. He simply offered her tea, which she accepted, and the use of his forge, which she also accepted. She worked alone for the two hours she had asked for. Kajiro sat outside and did not look in. He heard sounds from within that he described later as "what I imagine lightning sounds like when it is deciding where to fall."

She left before he could offer more tea. She left the blade on the anvil, still warm.

Kajiro kept it for one day. He understood on some fundamental level that it was not for him — not because he was unworthy, but because the blade had been made with a specific recipient in mind, someone the Archon had foreseen, and keeping it would be like storing a letter addressed to someone else. He delivered it to the Shogunate the next morning.

The blade spent forty years in a vault before a general named Katsuragi Ren picked it up to examine it and the blade lit up so suddenly and so violently with purple lightning that three observers were briefly knocked unconscious by the EMP burst. Katsuragi was unharmed. The blade had simply recognized her.

She carried it for twenty-two years, through fourteen campaigns, including the defense of Narukami Island against an assault force three times the size of her own. The assault force lost eighty percent of its soldiers before retreat. Katsuragi's force lost six.

When she died, the blade went dark. It has not lit up again. It is waiting.

**Tagline:** *"The Archon did not forge a weapon. She forged a recognition — a blade that knows its wielder before they know themselves."*

### SWORD 10 — PROTOTYPE RANCOUR
**Element:** Geo (Gold/Brown)
**Rarity:** ★★★★ (4 Star)
**Origin:** Liyue — The Blackcliff Forge
**ATK:** 44 | **Substat:** PHYS DMG +7.5%

#### The Story

The Blackcliff Forge is not a place that welcomes visitors. It exists in the mountains north of Liyue Harbor in a location that does not appear on official maps, staffed by smiths who are referred to in Liyue administrative records only as "contractors" and whose names are not recorded anywhere publicly accessible.

What the Blackcliff Forge makes is not for sale to the public.

Prototype Rancour is classified, in the records of the forge, as a "terminated development project." It was the seventh iteration of an attempt to create a blade that could absorb and redirect the kinetic energy of incoming attacks — not block them, but use them. The first six iterations either failed to absorb anything, absorbed too much and became dangerously unstable, or were destroyed in testing.

The seventh iteration worked. The problem was that it worked too well.

In controlled testing, a moderate strike against the blade produced an output force nearly four times the input. An uncontrolled test — when a panicked junior researcher accidentally swung a heavy hammer at it from close range — produced an output force that put a dent in the reinforced stone wall of the testing chamber. The wall was forty centimeters of compressed rock. The dent was twelve centimeters deep.

The project was terminated. The blade was decommissioned. It was supposed to be melted down.

It was not melted down. Someone — no one admits who — decided that melting it down was inadvisable, given the uncertainty about what would happen to the absorbed and stored kinetic energy when the metal reached forge temperature. Instead, it was placed in a sealed containment vault where it would be "monitored indefinitely."

"Indefinitely" lasted nineteen years before the vault was opened during a facility reorganization and someone picked it up.

That person is no longer employed at the Blackcliff Forge and also no longer lives in Liyue. They left voluntarily. The blade is listed as "whereabouts unknown" in current facility records.

No one at the Blackcliff Forge talks about this. No one at the Blackcliff Forge talks about anything, actually. That is part of why they are not on the official maps.

**Tagline:** *"The forge classified it as terminated. The blade disagreed."*

## Atmosphere Notes for Development

These are not technical specs — they are feeling guides for anyone making visual decisions:

- **The vault feels ancient.** Stone that has never seen sunlight. Air that has not moved in centuries. The smoke is not threatening — it is the breath of waiting things.

- **The swords are the only light source.** Everything else is shadow. This is intentional. The world recedes; the blades remain.

- **The camera moves like a reverent visitor.** Not fast, not jerky. Slow. Deliberate. Like someone who knows they are in a place that deserves respect.

- **The music, if added, should feel like memory.** Not triumphant. Not sad. Something that sounds like standing in a place where important things happened a long time ago.

- **Each element has a temperature.** Electro is sharp and cool. Anemo is light and ancient. Hydro is deep and clear. Pyro is warm and alive. Cryo is still and vast. Geo is heavy and permanent. The lighting for each section should carry these temperatures.

- **The Dull Blade section is different.** Everything else is epic. The Dull Blade section is quiet. Smaller text. Simpler lighting. Almost no particles. This is intentional — its power is in its ordinariness.

- **The Narukami section is the climax.** It is the last 5-star sword before the outro. The lighting should be the most dramatic. The camera angle the most cinematic. If there is one moment in the site that should feel like a game trailer — it is this one.
